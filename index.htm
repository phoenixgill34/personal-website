My Name is Kuldeep Gill, and I am a Ph.d Candidate at Worcester Polytechnic Institute, Worcester, USA.

I am currently pursuing my Ph.d at Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI) where I am a Research Assistant at Wireless Innovation Laboratory under Dr. Alexander Wyglinski. My research interests include Cognitive Radios, Dynamic Spectrum Access, Bio-inspired Spectrum Access Techniques, Vehicular Communication. In past I have worked as an Automotive Engineer at Robert Bosch designing where my main responsibilities included designing .hex codes for North American OEM. I was also a Teaching Assistant at WPI for ECE introduction course and my responsibilities include assisting students in their projects and helping them in Lab.

Experience

Research Assistant,Wireless Innovation Laboratory,WPI

Designed Bumblebee inspired metaheuristic algorithm for optimal channel selection in vehicular network in collaboration with Biologists from WPI. Implemented Cooperative Spectrum Sensing test-bed for heterogeneous networks using USRP N210 and RTL- SDR radios in the laboratory and compared both soft and hard data fusion schemes. Designed a channel for high speed railways in a tunnel environment by mathematically deriving the time-series K-factor for Rician channel model in order to test the HSR performance. Currently working with a team of undergraduates to perform the field measurements for Bumblebee algorithm in a vehicular networking environment using Analog Devices Pluto software-defined radio.

Automotive Engineer, Robert Bosch, India

Teaching Assistant, WPI

Teaching Assistant, Wireless and Networking Courses. Main responsibility included taking care of the labs and designing the laboratory report for the students.
